۱. صرف اینکه یک شرکت از ارم یک شرکت خارجی یا پرچم یک کشور دیگر، یا در سایتش لینک آدرس یک شرکت دیگر را گذاشت، یا در کاتالوگش استفاده نمود، دلیل بر نمایندگی واقعی آن نمی گردد.
۲. عکس سلفی گرفتن و ساخت کلیپ و جنجال رسانه ای راه انداختن، دلیل بر رزومه واقعی یک شخص یا شرکت نمی باشد. هوشیار باشیم و گول ظواهر و تبلیغات را نخوریم.
۳. اسم کامل شرکت ها همراه با سابقه آنها از طریق سایت "روزنامه رسمی" (با همین نام در گوگل سرچ بفرمائید) قابل سرچ و استعلام گیری می باشد، بنابراین پیش از تحت تاثیر قرار گرفتن رزومه فیک برخی از شرکت ها، به سادگی از چند مرکز معتبر درباره این شرکت ها، آدرس واقعی آنها، موسس آنها، رزومه اجرایی پیشین آنها و ... جستجوی لازمه را به عمل آورید.
۴. معمولا شرکت های طراحی خارجی مانند نیوزیلندی، ایتالیایی، انگلیسی، کانادایی و ... از فعالیت و ادعاهای واقعی نمایندگان خود (اگر واقعا نماینده آن شرکت ها در ایران باشند!) اطلاعی ندارند، بنابراین همواره توصیه اکید میگردد که برای ریزترین موارد و اخذ استعلام های لازمه با شرکت های مادر در ارتباط باشید. بعضا مشاهده می شود که از اعتبار یک شرکت مطرح استفاده شده، پروژه به اسم آن شرکت جلو رفته و در نهایت پروژه به سمت استفاده از اجناس کم کیفیت چینی و ترکیه ای هدایت می گردد، بنابراین در این موارد هوشیاری لازمه را همیشه به خرج دهید.
۵. همیشه اخذ استعلام ها را بر اساس سند و مدرک مکتوب طبق درخواست رسمی با سربرگ رسمی آن شرکت از آنها درخواست نمایید، نه شفاهی و تلفنی.

Since the structure is simple, it can be inspected by visual inspection.
3. It is safe even if an earthquake occurs several decades later
Since the characteristics of lead do not change over time, the specified performance can be achieved even in the event of an earthquake after a long period of installation. (Performance changes when energy is absorbed)
4. Recyclable material
It can be replaced after an earthquake.

Platinum Functionality Criteria, mandatory for Seismic Risk Categories IV buildings: R= 1.0; the maximum lateral story drifts shall not exceed 0.0030 times the story height; and the median value of the 5% damped floor spectra accelerations of the occupied stories, for the period range from 0.05 to 3 seconds, shall not exceed 0.3g as determined by Response History Procedure performed in accordance with ASCE 7-16 17.6.3.4 and this Standard for Continued Functionality. For all non-structural components throughout the structure, the horizontal seismic design force Fp taken as a fixed value of 0.4 Wp. These criteria intend that for buildings having structural and architectural details that are compliant with ASCE 7-16 and this Standard for Continued Functionality, shaking damage to architectural components will be less than 2% of the building replacement cost, consistent with the REDi Platinum Scenario Expected Loss.
Gold Functionality Criteria, mandatory for Seismic Risk Categories III buildings: R= 1.25; the maximum lateral story drifts shall not exceed 0.0045 times the story height; and the median value of the 5% damped floor spectra accelerations of the occupied stories, for the period range from 0.05 to 3 seconds, shall not exceed 0.4g as determined by Response History Procedure performed in accordance with ASCE 7-16 17.6.3.4 and this Standard for Continued Functionality. For all non-structural components throughout the structure, the horizontal seismic design force Fp taken as a fixed value of 0.6 Wp. These criteria intend that for buildings having structural and architectural details that are compliant with ASCE 7-16 and this Standard for Continued Functionality, damage to architectural components will be less than 4% of the building replacement cost, consistent with the REDi Gold Scenario Expected Loss.
Silver Functionality Criteria, mandatory for Seismic Risk Categories I and II buildings: R= 1.5; the maximum lateral story drifts shall not exceed 0.0060 times the story height; and the median value of the 5% damped floor spectra accelerations of the occupied stories, for the period range from 0.05 to 3 seconds, shall not exceed 0.6g as determined by Response History Procedure performed in accordance with ASCE 7-16 17.6.3.4 and this SISC. For all non-structural components throughout the structure, the horizontal seismic design force Fp taken as a fixed value of 0.8 Wp. These criteria intend that for buildings having typical structural and architectural details that are compliant with ASCE 7-16 and this Standard for Continued Functionality, seismic shaking damage to architectural components will be less than 8% of the building replacement cost, consistent with the REDi Silver Scenario Expected Loss.
Buildings with or without seismic isolators or dampers are in compliance with this Standard for Continued Functionality when structural and seismic analyses as specified herein shown them to comply with the criteria above. These Continued Functionality criteria do not apply when seismic isolators are used for the life-safety retrofit to reduce collapse risks of existing structures. A Rotational Friction Damper dissipates seismic energy by friction generated through a rotating friction joint, with constant torque. The force in the connected braces is dependent on the angle to overcome the slip force in the friction joint. The component of the force in the brace, normal to the link multiplied by the length of the link must be equal or greater than the constant torque to slip. This type of damper can be modeled with a rectangular hysteretic loop for small displacements (changes in angle between the damper links).
rotational friction damper variation

Rotational Friction Dampers have been used since the early 1900s in vehicle suspensions and represent a robust technology for use in seismic force resisting systems for structures. Although this is a well proven technology there is more variation in the force due to changes in angle as well as variance in individual dampers due to manufacturing tolerances, it therefore remains important to conduct 100% testing of the rotational friction dampers before use in seismic applications.

For small building drifts, a rotational friction damper can be assumed to have a rectangular hysteretic loop which make analysis and design simple and straightforward. For larger drifts (rotations in the damper links) more over strength in the connections will be needed and the hysteretic loop should be explicitly modelled as trapezoidal in a non linear analysis. This effect is illustrated in the characteristic trapezoidal loop (bottom left) of a single rotational friction damper or the parallelogram shaped loop (bottom right) of the x-brace rotational damper. Depending on the aspect ratio and requirements of the specific frame the variations can be reduced. In cases where the force variations must be minimized (e.g. most retrofits) please see our inline friction damper.
